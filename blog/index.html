Projects:

  1. Mind Rhymes:

    Mind rhymes are a subset of the broader category of rhyme subversion, where the intended rhyming target is replaced by a new word or phrase. For example, the 1948 song "Shaving Cream" by Benny Bell displays the following pattern:

    "I have a sad story to tell you./
    It may hurt your feelings a bit. /
    Last night I walked into my bathroom, /
    and stepped in a big pile of _____ ."

    What fills the blank? Our knowledge of context, rhyme scheme, metrical structure, and other linguistic factors suggest that the blank should be filled by shit:

    "... and stepped in a big pile of shit!"

    However, something else is overtly realized in the song itself:

    "... and stepped in a big pile of shaving cream!"

    My work attempts to understand what licenses these mind rhymes using experimental and information-theoretic measures. Further, I am interested in the question of how humans and models recover the intended rhyming target upon hearing the subversion. This project is ongoing.

  2. Syllables and Nasals within the Languages of the DRC

    Moving away from experimental and computational work, I study the phonological patterning of nasals and syllables of the languages spoken in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC). I aim to capture typological and areal variation within these languages through the use of Optimality-Theory (OT) constraints. This projet is ongoing.
