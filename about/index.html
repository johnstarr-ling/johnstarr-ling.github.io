Hi, I'm John R. Starr!

I am a second-year PhD student at Cornell University, where I am studying Linguistics with a minor in Cognitive Science.

My primary research interests lie within the interfaces of linguistics, cognitive science, and natural language processing. At the moment, I am researching linguistic phenomena that require divergent processing of a single linguistic signal, such as puns and rhyme subversions (mind rhymes), and comparing how humans and computational models vary with in their processing of these items.

From a more theoretical linguistics perspective, I study the phonological patterning of nasals and syllables within languages of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

I am a member of the Cornell University Computational Psycholinguistics Lab, or CPsyd for short. I am advised by the director of the lab, Marten van Schijndel.
